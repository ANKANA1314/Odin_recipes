khichdi

Description

Khichdi is a classic and comforting Indian dish known for its simplicity, nutritional value, and versatility. It is a one-pot meal made by cooking rice and lentils together along with various spices and vegetables. This humble dish has a rich history and is a popular choice across the diverse regions of India.

Ingredients

  • rice
  • masoor dal
  • water
  • ghee or vegetable oil
  • cumin seeds
  • mustard seeds
  • tumeric powder
  • safoetida
  • green chillies
  • ginger
  • tomato
  • mixed vegetables
  • salt
  • coriander leaves

Steps

  1. Wash and soak the rice and dal: Rinse the rice and dal together under cold water until the water runs clear. Then, soak them in water for about 30 minutes. This step is optional but can help reduce the cooking time.
  2. Prepare the vegetables (if using): Chop the mixed vegetables into small pieces. You can use a combination of carrots, peas, cauliflower, or any other vegetables you like.
  3. In a pressure cooker or a large pot, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds (if using). Let them splutter and release their aroma. Add chopped onions (if using) and sauté until they turn golden brown. Stir in the grated ginger and slit green chilies. Sauté for a minute or until the raw smell disappears. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy. Drain the soaked rice and dal and add them to the pot. Mix well with the sautéed ingredients. Add turmeric powder, asafoetida, and salt to taste. Mix everything together. Pour in 3 cups of water and add the chopped vegetables (if using). Stir well to combine.
  4. If using a pressure cooker, close the lid and cook on medium heat for 2 whistles. Then, reduce the heat to low and let it cook for another 5 minutes. Turn off the heat and allow the pressure to release naturally
  5. Once the khichdi is cooked, gently fluff it with a fork. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
