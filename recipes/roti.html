Roti

Description

Roti is a traditional Indian flatbread that holds a special place in the hearts and meals of people across the country. It is a staple food in India and is widely enjoyed as a versatile accompaniment to various curries, vegetables, and lentil dishes.

Ingredients

  • whole wheat flour
  • water
  • salt

Steps

  1. Mix the Flour and Salt: In a mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and a pinch of salt. Mix the dry ingredients together using a spoon or your fingers.
  2. Add Water Gradually: Slowly add water to the flour mixture, a little at a time, while kneading the dough with your hand. Continue adding water until you achieve a soft and smooth dough. The exact amount of water needed may vary, so be careful not to add too much water at once.
  3. Knead the Dough: Once the dough starts coming together, transfer it to a clean surface and knead it for about 5-7 minutes. The kneading process helps develop gluten in the dough, which makes the roti soft and elastic. The dough should not be too sticky or too dry.
  4. Rest the Dough: Cover the dough with a damp cloth or place it in a covered bowl. Allow it to rest for about 15-20 minutes. This resting period relaxes the gluten and makes the dough easier to work with.
  5. Divide the Dough: After resting, divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls. The size of the balls will determine the size of your rotis.
  6. Roll the Roti: Take one dough ball and flatten it slightly with your palm. Dip it into dry flour and then use a rolling pin to roll it into a thin, round disc. Rotate the roti frequently while rolling to maintain an even shape.
  7. Cook the Roti: Heat a tawa (flat griddle) or a non-stick pan over medium heat. Place the rolled roti on the hot tawa and cook it for about 30 seconds to 1 minute on one side. You will notice small bubbles forming on the surface.
  8. Flip and Cook: Once you see bubbles, flip the roti to the other side using tongs or a spatula. Cook for another 30 seconds to 1 minute, pressing lightly with a cloth or spatula to help it puff up.
  9. Cook Over Open Flame (Optional): If you prefer, you can finish cooking the roti directly over an open flame. Using tongs, place the partially cooked roti on the flame. It will puff up and develop a slight char on the surface. Be careful not to burn it.
  10. Brush with Ghee or Oil (Optional): For added flavor, you can brush the cooked roti with ghee or oil on one side.
  11. Repeat: Repeat the rolling and cooking process for the remaining dough balls until you have made all the rotis.
  12. Serve: Serve the hot and freshly cooked rotis with your favorite curry, vegetable dish, or any other accompaniment of your choice.
